The Astronomy group would like to increase interactions with our undergraduate majors and minors. This page lists resources (both local and national) available to undergraduates with an interest in astronomy.

Information on the undergraduate major and minor can be found on the academics page.

Local Events of Interest to Astronomy Undergrads

Seminars and Journal Club

Anyone with an interest in Astronomy is welcomed to attend our (semi-)weekly Journal Club and Seminars.

In Journal Club, faculty, grad students, and sometimes undergraduates present an overview of a recent research paper to the rest of the Astronomy group. There are typically 1-2 presentations per week, and these are very informal. This is a good way to learn about the latest developments in all fields of Astronomy.

For our Seminars, we bring guest speakers from around the country to talk to us about their research. Typically, we pick speakers with research interests aligned with those of the different researchers in our group.

The current schedules for both Journal Club and Astronomy Seminars can always be found from the links off the main events portion of Astronomy group webpage.

Colloquia

Undergrads are also encouraged to attend the weekly Physics and Astronomy Departmental Colloquia, every Tues. starting at 4:15pm in Harriman 137 (cookies and coffee begin at 3:45pm). There are typically several astronomy-related talks each semester. (Don't worry if you don't understand everything in the talk—not all the faculty present will either! You will certainly pick up something new from the talk.)

Information About Applying to Grad School

There is a lot of information on the web about applying for graduate schools. Most schools will require the Physics GREs. A lot of resources for the exam (practice tests, tips, etc.) can be found by searching online. Below we list a few of them.

Tips on Preparing for the Physics GRE
A blog post by a GRE question-writer on how to prepare for the Physics GRE exam.
Official Physics GRE page
The official page for the Physics GRE, including a practice test
Local SPS Resources on GREs
Notes collected by the Stony Brook chapter of the Society of Physics Students on the Physics GREs.

There are also a lot of resources to help with choosing a graduate program. Perhaps the best resource is to ask one of the Astronomy faculty here at Stony Brook for advice. About every 10 years, the National Research Council ranks graduate programs, including Astronomy and Astrophysics departments and Physics departments. These rankings can also be helpful when picking a school.

Graduate School Rankings
A site that shows rankings of graduate programs using National Research Council evaluations of graduate programs.

 

External Research Opportunities

There are a number of programs available for undergraduates interested in doing summer research in astronomy. Here we provide links to the application pages for these programs as they become available.

Harvard Origins of Life Initiative
Summer Undergraduate Research Grants of up to $5,000 are available for undergraduates working in Origins team member labs on Origins-related projects.
NSF Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU)
The National Science Foundation (NSF) provides various astronomy programs across the country with funding to provide paid summer research internships for undergraduates. This is a great opportunity to spend your summer in another part of the country performing astronomy research. Note that the deadlines for applications are in Dec/Jan for the following summer.
