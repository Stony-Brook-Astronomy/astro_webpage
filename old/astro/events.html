Astronomy Seminars

The Astronomy Seminars take place on Wed. at 1:30pm in ESS 450.

Joint Stony Brook/Brookhaven Cosmology Seminars

The Joint SB/BNL Cosmology Seminars take place biweekly with hosting alternating between SB and BNL. When hosted at SB, the seminars are on select Wednesdays at 1:30pm in ESS 450. When hosted at BNL, they are on select Thursdays at 3pm in the small seminar room in building 510. The SB seminar is followed by a wine and cheese discussion in ESS 437, and the BNL seminar by a discussion over tea and cookies in the large seminar room lounge.

Astronomy Open Nights

The Astronomy Open Night is a public lecture series that takes place generally on the first Friday of each month during the semester (starting at 7:30pm in ESS 001). If the weather is good, viewing through our roof-top telescope follows the lecture.

Public Observing

The Undergraduate Astronomy Club occassionally has public viewing. See the club's webpage for details and location.

Journal Club

The Astronomy Journal Club meets each Friday during the semester at 12:50pm in ESS 450.

Physics and Astronomy Colloquia

The Physics and Astronomy colloquia take place on Tues. afternoons, at 4:15pm in Harriman 137. Coffee and cookies are available starting at 3:45pm.

Astro-ph Discussion

The Astro-ph Discussion takes place each Wednesday morning at 10:30am in the common room (ESS 437). The Astronomy group meets to discuss new papers posted that week on the astro-ph arXiv, organized on the Stony Brook Vox Charta page.

Astronomy Coffee

Astronomy coffee is an informal weekly meeting of the group on Thurs. mornings at 10:30am in the common room, ESS 437.
