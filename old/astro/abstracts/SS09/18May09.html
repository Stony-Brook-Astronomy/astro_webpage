Title : Lessons Learned from the Infrared, and Future Prospects for Studies of Nearby Galaxies
Speaker : Daniela Calzetti, University of Massachusetts @ Amherst

I will review recent results, and the lessons learned from them, on the properties of star formation in nearby galaxies, using multi-wavelength datasets, from the ultraviolet (GALEX) to the infrared (Spitzer). I will place these results in the context of future developments in the field, where the exploitation of upcoming facilities, including Herschel, the Large Millimeter Telescope, and ALMA will be key to address a number of unsolved issues.
