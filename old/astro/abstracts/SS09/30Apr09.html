Title : The Role of Gas in the Evolution of Early Type Galaxies
Speaker : Jennifer Donovan, Columbia University

Between z~1 and the present day, the stellar masses of early type galaxies have doubled, and since z~2, their physical sizes have increased by at least the same factor. Recent work has addressed the viability of dry, or dissipationless, mergers to explain this evolution in mass and size. However, many local field early type galaxies are rich in neutral gas, and ultraviolet colors indicate that many of these systems contain a significant component of recently formed stars. I will address a set of optical criteria which have been used to select dry mergers, describe some conditions under which early types turn their gas into stars, and explore how common this phenomenon is.
