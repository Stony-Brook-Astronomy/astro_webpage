2011

  • Dr. Aren Heinze joined the astronomy group as a postdoc. He will lead the analysis of the data from the Spitzer Cycle 8 Exploration Science Program "Weather on Other Worlds." (12/2011)
     
  • Astronomy faculty Calder and Zingale are part of 2 teams that were recently awarded major allocations on DOE supercomputers through the INCITE program. A combined amount of 86 million CPU hours were awarded to their collaborations to study Type Ia supernovae. (11/16/2011)
     
  • The Astronomy faculty invite all undergraduate majors and minors to our Town Meeting on Wed. Nov. 9th to discuss Astronomy courses, research opportunities, the Astronomy club, and graduate school. (11/01/2011)

  • A international team led by Prof. Metchev was awarded 873 hours with the Spitzer Space Telescope to conduct one of ten large Exploration Science programs, entitled "Weather on Other Worlds." The program will study weather patterns on brown dwarf and exoplanetary worlds by searching for minute brightness changes caused by irregular cloud covers. (05/2011)
