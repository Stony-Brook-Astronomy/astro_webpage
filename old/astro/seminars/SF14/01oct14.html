Accretion physics of X-ray binary outbursts
Phil Armitage (U. of Colorado/JILA)

X-ray binaries exhibit a rich array of variability and outburst behavior. Most of this can be well-characterized in terms of cyclic changes between geometrically thin and thick disks, but the origin of these changes is poorly understood at a more fundamental level. In this talk I will discuss physical models of outbursts in two classes of system. For black hole X-ray binaries that display state transitions, I will argue that cyclic accumulation of magnetic flux near the black hole can explain the hysteresis seen in the timing of transitions between thin and thick disk states. For Be / X-ray binaries, simulations suggest that periodic excitation of disk eccentricity is responsible for the pattern of large and small accretion outbursts. In both cases, it appears that established properties of magnetized accretion flows, when combined with known gravitational dynamics in binary systems, have promise to explain what is observed.
