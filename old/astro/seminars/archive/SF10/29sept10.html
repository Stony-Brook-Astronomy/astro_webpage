Title : The Formation of Molecular Clouds and Massive Stars
Speaker : Allison Crocker, UMASS

Recent results of UV, optical spectoscopy, mm and radio observations reveal that a significant fraction of early-type (E and S0) galaxies host reservoirs of cool gas and recent and/or ongoing star formation. But are the properties of star formation and cool gas the same in these bulge-dominated galaxies as in the more commonly studied spiral, starburst and irregular star-forming galaxies? First I will show what a handful of these star-forming early-type galaxies look like in detail, with CO and HI maps for the cold gas and optical spectroscopy and UV maps to detect young stars. Many commonly used star formation indicators fail or are less reliable in E and S0 galaxies than in the spirals and starbursts against which they were calibrated. I report on the mismatch between several of these tracers including the FIR, 24 micron, 8 micron PAH, radio and H-alpha. But despite these problems, the E and S0 galaxies surprisingly lie almost entirely within the large scatter of the Schmidt-Kennicutt law when the normal SF conversions are used. I also report on preliminary studies of the properties of molecular gas in these galaxies, using the high density tracers HCN, HCO+ and 13CO.
