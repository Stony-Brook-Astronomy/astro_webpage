Title : Recurrent Novae as Progenitors of Type Ia Supernovae Speaker : Brad Schaefer, LSU

The identity of the progenitor systems for Type Ia supernovae has for four decades been one of the premier problems in astrophysics, and its importance has only been amplified in the last decade with the requirement of knowing the progenitor for all dreams of precision supernova cosmology. One of the best candidates for the progenitor are recurrent novae, RNe, close binaries where Roche lobe overflow pours matter onto a white dwarf, with the nova eruptions coming roughly every few decades. However, there are some problems ("Are there enough RNe to produce the observed rate?" and "Is the white dwarf gaining mass?"). In this talk, I will present new and convincing answers to this two problems.
