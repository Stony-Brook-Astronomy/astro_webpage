Title : Star Formation in Unexpected Places -- The Ultraviolet View of Galaxy Evolution Provided by GALEX
Speaker : David Thilker (Johns Hopkins University)

GALEX has revolutionized the study of star formation in nearby galaxies, fully realizing the potential of UV as a SFR tracer directly linked to the photospheric emission of hot stars rather than the ISM. Much of the new UV insight has focused on low density / low surface brightness environments previously thought to be quiescent in terms of star formation. I will review the main discoveries stemming from GALEX survey observations. In particular, I will describe ongoing investigations pertaining to extended UV-disk (XUV-disk) galaxies, star-forming early type galaxies, low surface brightness galaxies (both large and small), tidal dwarfs, and intergalactic gaseous debris.
