Title : Rare Views of Ordinary Star Forming Galaxies at High Redshift
Speaker : Brenda Frye (University of San Francisco)

Understanding the evolution of star forming galaxies up to early cosmic times is an important problem in astrophysics. By undertaking galaxy redshift surveys in the fields of massive galaxy clusters, we are identifying the brightest examples of ordinary star forming galaxies back to when the universe was only 900 million years old. Our sample is bright enough to acquire spectroscopy that spatially resolves individual stellar populations along the disks. These studies are made possible by the combination of telescopes bound to the Earth plus large gravitational telescopes. In concert with large statistical studies of galaxy ensemble properties, these rare studies of individual objects are leading to a better understanding of the physics of baryonic mass assembly and the distribution of dark matter.
