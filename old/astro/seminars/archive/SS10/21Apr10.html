Title : "Classical Aspects of the Dark Matter and Dark Energy Problems"
Speaker : Philip Mannheim, UConn

We discuss why it is thought that there are copious amounts of dark matter and dark energy in the universe and why this is problem for the standard gravitational theory. We identify the freedom still present in classical gravitational theory that might enable us to dispense with dark matter and dark energy altogether. In particular we explore the alternate conformal gravity theory originally introduced by Weyl. We show that it provides fitting to a wide variety of galactic rotation curves without the need for dark matter, and provides fitting to the accelerating universe data without dark energy. We discuss the challenges that the conformal theory still faces. In a related YITP Seminar talk, "Quantum aspects of the Dark Matter and Dark Energy Problems", to be given on Thursday April 22 at 2:30pm in Math Tower 6-125, we will discuss how quantizing conformal gravity enables us to both construct a consistent quantum gravity theory and solve the cosmological constant problem.
