Title : "The Dynamics of Exoplanets from Direct Images to Transits"
Speaker : Daniel Fabrycky, Harvard

Over the past few years the radial velocity method of extrasolar planet detection has been supplemented by other methods. Direct images (for planets far from their stars) and transits (for planets close in) are giving us more intimate knowledge of the physical properties of these planets and are providing more ammunition for dynamical studies. The newly imaged multi-planet system HR 8799 is required by dynamical stability arguments to have either resonant orbits or very low planetary masses. In another system, torque from a distant ("directly imaged") binary star companion could explain the extreme eccentricity (0.93) of transiting planet HD 80606b; that theory predicted the spin of the host star would be misaligned from the planetary orbit, which was later confirmed spectroscopically. Such measurements among transiting planets suggest several migration mechanisms account for hot Jupiters. Finally, transit timing measurements are ruling out resonant companions to hot Jupiters down to absurdly low masses and thus may be poised to discover numerous resonant planetary systems using Kepler photometry.
