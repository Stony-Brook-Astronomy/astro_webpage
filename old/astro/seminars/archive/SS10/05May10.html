Title : "Outer Disk Gas and Star Formation in the Local Universe"
Speaker : Jennifer Donovan Meyer, Stony Brook

Studies of galaxy evolution are still working to explain the galaxy population that we observe at z=0. One aspect of this local population that is not yet fully understood is the existence of star forming regions in outer galactic disks. The existence of extended disk star formation has been known for many years, but one of the major discoveries made with the GALEX satellite has been the frequency with which it occurs. Even more recently, GALEX has revealed extended regions of star formation around some early-type galaxies, systems long believed to be at their evolutionary endpoint. Determining whether these red galaxies are being rejuvenated and turning blue has important implications for galaxy evolution, and determining the mode of star formation in these largely sub-Kennicutt regions -- and whether the IMF of the stars formed is universal -- has important implications for what we know about how stars form. I will discuss some recent results in this exciting field.
