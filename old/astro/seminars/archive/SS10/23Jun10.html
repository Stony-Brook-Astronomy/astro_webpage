Title : "Gravitational wave astronomy and BH-NS mergers: Astrophysical mystery to multitool"
Speaker : Richard O'Shaughnessy (PSU)

By the midpoint of the decade (2015), second-generation gravitational-wave detectors will regularly uncover the signature of at least several tens of few stellar mass compact object mergers each year. Many of these mergers will involve the disruption and capture of neutron stars into O(few) stellar mass black holes. In this talk I review how direct gravitational wave observations of these merger events, often combined with coincident electromagnetic signals, can constrain a wide variety of astrophysical processes: the life, death, and interactions of massive stars, particularly at low metallicity; the formation of stars and clusters to moderate redshift; the properties of nuclear matter; the nature of gravity in the strong field; and even the potential for weak gravitational parity violation.
