Title:Debris Disk Dynamics: A Key for Finding Hidden Exoplanets
Speaker: Marc Kuchner (NASA Goddard)

Extrasolar planets sculpt the debris disks around Beta Pictoris, HR 8799 and probably Fomalhaut. Many other circumstellar disks likely contain planets we could locate if we could better read their dynamical signatures in the circumstellar dust. I'll describe some recent progress in the development of this powerful planet-finding technique that's just coming into fruition: new 3-D models of debris disk dynamics that incorporate both collisions and resonant trapping of dust for the first time. These new techniques, coupled with high-contrast imaging on large telescopes, will allow us to interpret planetary signatures imprinted on a whole new range of disks. This method is probably the only way to find true Neptune analogs and the youngest Earth analogs orbiting nearby stars.
