Title: X-ray Scattering in the IGM
Speaker: Lia Corrales (Columbia)

X-rays offer a unique and direct method to probe dust in the interstellar and intergalactic medium.  Small angle scattering through dust grains produces a diffuse `halo' image around X-ray point sources, which has been observed as a result of interstellar dust in the plane of the Milky Way.  I will review the exciting prospects for observing X-ray scattering through dust in the intergalactic medium.  Dust dispersal mechanisms will preferentially destroy small grains, leading to a population of large `grey' intergalactic dust that affects cosmological SNe surveys in both the optical and infrared.  Quantifying the amount of dust in the IGM also offers information about the cosmic history of star formation and galactic feedback mechanisms.  I will describe how X-ray scattering images can yield insight towards the nature of IGM `clumps' such as DLAs and other absorption line systems.
