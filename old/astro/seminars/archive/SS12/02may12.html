Novae and Supernovae in Wide Binaries
Speaker: Jennifer L. Sokoloski (Columbia)

Approximately 1% of white dwarfs somehow become type Ia supernovae (SNIa). But how do they gain enough material to explode? One of the easiest progenitor scenarios to test is that of a wide binary in which a white dwarf accretes from a red-giant companion. I will review the strong evidence both for and against such wide binaries as progenitors of SNIa, and the resulting implication that the progenitors of SNIa could be diverse. In addition, since mass expulsion in nova eruptions is one of the main obstacles for an accreting white dwarf on its way to becoming a SNIa, I will also describe recent observations of novae that reveal so many surprises and conundrums that it is fair to say that the amount of mass ejected from novae, and thus the degree to which novae prevent a white dwarf from approaching the Chandrasekhar limit and experiencing a SNIa, is still an open question.
