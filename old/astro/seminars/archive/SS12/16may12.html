Giant Molecular Clouds in Nearby Spiral Galaxies
Speaker: Jennifer Donovan Meyer (SBU)

Resolved observations of molecular gas in nearby galaxies lend insight into many of the processes involved in galaxy evolution, as studies of the evolution of galaxies from high redshift to z=0, the evolution of spiral galaxy structure, and star formation within giant molecular clouds are all anchored on these observations. I will present and place in context recent observations of CO (J=1-0), the most commonly used tracer of molecular hydrogen, in the centers of four nearby spiral galaxies and discuss the properties of the GMCs located in these regions.
