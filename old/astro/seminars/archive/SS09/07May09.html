Title : The complex history of massive galaxies
Speaker : Pieter van Dokkum, Yale University

In galaxy formation models massive galaxies, such as giant elliptical galaxies, are relatively recent arrivals in the galaxy population: these models predict that they were built up in many generations of mergers, continuing to the present day. Recent studies with large telescopes on the ground and the Hubble Space Telescope have provided direct information on the evolution of these objects with redshift. The talk will discuss these results, and outline avenues for further progress.
