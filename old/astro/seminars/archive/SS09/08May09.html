Title : Measuring dark energy with LISA
Speaker : Bernard Schutz, Max Plank Institute fur Gravitational Physics @ Golm

The space-based gravitational wave detector LISA is a joint project of NASA and ESA, which could be launched in about 10 years. In contrast with ground-based detectors, LISA will observe important sources with very high sensitivity to redshifts of 15 or 20. Mergers of a pair of supermassive black holes are standard candles: from each observation, LISA will be able to determine the distance (cosmologically, a luminosity distance) to the system with accuracies of a few percent or better, completely independently of the various cosmological distance ladders. Simultaneous and follow-up optical and X-ray observations have a good chance of identifying the host galaxy for the event, so that over the lifetime of LISA can provide an independent check on cosmological distances, on the reliability of other standard candles, and on the measurements on cosmological parameters. LISA should provide a measurement of the dark energy equation of state parameter, and even has a chance of constraining its evolution in time out to redshift 2 or greater.
