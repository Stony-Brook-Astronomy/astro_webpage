Title: A Zoo of Extrasolar Planet(esimal) Compositions Derived From Externally-Polluted White Dwarfs
Speaker: Ben Zuckerman (UCLA)

Study of extrasolar planetary systems has provided a wealth of surprises, not the least of which is the realization that the photospheres of many white dwarf stars are the graveyards for rocky bodies that once orbited these stars. The photospheres present a tableau on which the elemental compositions of these erstwhile asteroids or rocky planets are displayed. There appear both similarities and differences between the compositions of extrasolar rocky bodies and those of more familiar solar system objects (e.g. bulk Earth). Collisions between large rocky objects are indicated in some of these planetary systems. Echelle spectra obtained with the Keck telescope at Mauna Kea Observatory are so sensitive that, for example, they reveal the uncommon element scandium in 3 heavily-polluted white dwarfs; in the Sun, scandium is a billion times less abundant than hydrogen. These spectral studies have shown that >~30% of white dwarfs are orbited by ancient planetary systems that contain rocky objects and major planets.
