Title: Why Don't Massive Stars Like to Blow-up?: Numerical Simulation and Core Collapse Supernovae
Speaker: Sean Couch (University of Chicago)

The explosive deaths of massive stars are observed everyday. It is an empirical fact that stars more massive than about eight solar masses end their short lives by blowing-up as supernovae but, thus far, we lack a completely successful theory that can explain why. Sophisticated numerical simulations of stellar core collapse fail to find explosions for all but the least massive progenitor stars. When explosions are found in simulations, they are often under-energetic. I will review recent work on the criteria for successful core collapse supernovae, including the intriguing hypothesis that three-dimensional simulations are critical. I will discuss in detail our current efforts to simulate core collapse supernovae using FLASH and what ingredients may be missing from the theoretical supernova stew that may explain why robust numerical explosions have been so elusive.
