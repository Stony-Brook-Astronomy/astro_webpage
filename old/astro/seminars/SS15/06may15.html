Cosmological constraints from weak lensing non-Gaussian statistics
Jia Liu (Columbia University)

Weak gravitational lensing is one of the most promising techniques to probe dark energy with improved precision in the future. Our work to date suggests that the information in the nonlinear regime exceeds that in the two-point functions. I will discuss cosmological constraints from non-Gaussian statistics, using the publicly available CFHTLenS data, combined with a large suite of ray-tracing simulations. I will also introduce the utility of cross-correlating weak galaxy lensing with CMB lensing, a technique that will be useful as larger weak lensing surveys such as DES and LSST come online.
