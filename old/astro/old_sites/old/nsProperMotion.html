THE MOTION OF RX J185635-3754 -- THE NEAREST NEUTRON STAR TO EARTH

This photograph is the sum of three Hubble Space Telescope images. North is down, east is to the right. The image, taken by the Wide Field and Planetary Camera 2, is 8.8 seconds of arc across (west to east), and 6.6 seconds of arc top-to-bottom (south to north). An arc second is a unit of angular measure. There are 3,600 arc seconds in 1 degree and 360 degrees in a full circle.

All stars line up in this composite picture, except the neutron star, which moves across the image in a direction 10 degrees south of east. The three images of the neutron star are labeled by date. The proper motion is 1/3 of a second of arc per year. The small wobble caused by parallax (not visible in the image) has a size of 0.016 seconds of arc, giving a distance of 200 light-years.
