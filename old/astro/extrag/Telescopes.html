Telescopes

Stony Brook Radio Interferometer

Two-element interferometer being built at Stony Brook University; currently sensitive to the Sun and satellites emitting at 3cm


GALEX

Orbiting satellite sensitive to the near- and far-ultraviolet; both imaging and spectroscopy are available

Hubble Space Telescope

Orbiting satellite with 2.4-m mirror sensitive to wavelengths from the near-ultraviolet to the infrared; several cameras and spectrographs are available

Spitzer Space Telescope

Orbiting satellite with 0.85m mirror sensitive to infrared wavelengths from 3 to 180 microns; both imaging and spectroscopy are available

Herschel

Recently launched satellite with 3.5-m mirror sensitive to the far-infrared (55 to 672 microns)

CARMA

Radio array with 15 dishes consisting of the former BIMA and OVRO arrays (dish sizes 6.1-m and 10.4-m, respectively); sensitive to 1 mm, 3mm and 1 cm wavelengths

Nobeyama 45m (NRO45m)

Single dish radio telescope capable of observing with a single beam or 25 beams (BEARS array); sensitive to wavelengths from 1 mm to 1 cm

EVLA

Newly upgraded Expanded Very Large Array; radio array consisting of 27 25-m dishes, sensitive to wavelengths from 0.7 to 400 cm

Observatory at Scott Base, Antarctica

New Zealand’s research base in Antarctica; we use the facility to observe mm wave emission (278 GHz) of ClO from the atmosphere
